Consider two variables $\mathbf{x}$ and $\mathbf{y}$ having joint distribution $p(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y})$. Show that the differential entropy of this pair of variables satisfies
$$
\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}] \leqslant \mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}]+\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]
$$
with equality if, and only if, $\mathbf{x}$ and $\mathbf{y}$ are statistically independent.
$$\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}]=-\iint p(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}) \ln p(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}) \mathrm{d} \mathbf{y} \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}=\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y} \mid \mathbf{x}]+\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}]$$

$$\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y} \mid \mathbf{x}]=-\iint p(\mathbf{y}, \mathbf{x}) \ln p(\mathbf{y} \mid \mathbf{x}) \mathrm{d} \mathbf{y} \mathrm{d} \mathbf{x}$$

$$\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]=-\int p(\mathbf{y}) \ln p(\mathbf{y}) \mathrm{d} \mathbf{y}$$

$$\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}]=-\int p(\mathbf{x}) \ln p(\mathbf{x}) \mathrm{d} \mathbf{x}$$
then
$$
\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}] \leqslant \mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}]+\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]\equiv\mathrm{H}[y\mid\mathbf{x}]+\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}] \leqslant \mathrm{H}[\mathbf{x}]+\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]\equiv\mathrm{H}[y\mid\mathbf{x}] \leqslant\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]
$$
if $\mathbf{x}$ and $\mathbf{y}$ are statistically independent, then
$$\begin{aligned}\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y} \mid \mathbf{x}]&=-\iint p(\mathbf{y}, \mathbf{x}) \ln p(\mathbf{y} \mid \mathbf{x}) \mathrm{d} \mathbf{y} \mathrm{d} \mathbf{x} \\ &=-\iint p(\mathbf{y}) p(\mathbf{x}) \ln p(\mathbf{y}) \mathrm{d} \mathbf{y} \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}\\&=-\int p(\mathbf{y})\ln p(\mathbf{y}) \left(\int p(\mathbf{x}) \mathrm{d} \mathbf{x} \right)\mathrm{d} \mathbf{y}\\&=-\int p(\mathbf{y})\ln p(\mathbf{y})\mathrm{d} \mathbf{y}\\&=\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]\end{aligned}$$
if $\mathbf{x}$ and $\mathbf{y}$ are NOT statistically independent, then
$$\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]-\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}|\mathbf{x}] = \mathrm{I}[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}]\geq0$$

$$\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]-\mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}|\mathbf{x}] \geq0$$

$$ \mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}|\mathbf{x}] \leq \mathrm{H}[\mathbf{y}]$$

Derive an expression for the differential entropy of the variable $x=x_{1}+x_{2}$. To do this, first find the distribution of $x$ by using the relation
$$
p(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} p\left(x \mid x_{2}\right) p\left(x_{2}\right) \mathrm{d} x_{2}
$$
and completing the square in the exponent. Then observe that this represents the convolution of two Gaussian distributions, which itself will be Gaussian, and finally make use of the result (1.110) for the entropy of the univariate Gaussian.
$$\begin{aligned} p(x) &=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} p\left(x \mid x_{2}\right) p\left(x_{2}\right) \mathrm{d} x_{2} \\ &=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f\left(x \mid \mu_{1}+x_{2}, \tau_{1}\right) f\left(x_{2} \mid \mu_{2}, \tau_{2}\right) \mathrm{d} x_{2} \\ &=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{1}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}-x_{2}\right)^{2} / 2} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{2}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{2}\left(x_{2}-\mu_{2}\right)^{2} / 2} \mathrm{~d} x_{2}\\&=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{1}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)^{2} / 2} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{2}}{2 \pi}} e^{\left(-\tau_{2}\left(x_{2}-\mu_{2}\right)^{2}+2 \tau_{1} x_{2}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)-\tau_{1} x_{2}^{2}\right) / 2} \mathrm{~d} x_{2} \\
&=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{1}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)^{2} / 2} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{2}}{2 \pi}} e^{\left(\left(-\tau_{2}-\tau_{1}\right) x_{2}^{2}+\left(2 \tau_{2} \mu_{2}+2 \tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)\right) x_{2}-\tau_{2} \mu_{2}^{2}\right) / 2} \mathrm{~d} x_{2} \\
&=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\left(\sqrt{\frac{\tau_{1}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)^{2} / 2} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{2}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{2} \mu_{2}^{2} / 2}\right) e^{\left(\left(-\tau_{2}-\tau_{1}\right) x_{2}^{2}+2\left(\tau_{2} \mu_{2}+\tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)\right) x_{2}\right) / 2} \mathrm{~d} x_{2}\\
&=\left(\sqrt{\frac{\tau_{1}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)^{2} / 2} \sqrt{\frac{\tau_{2}}{2 \pi}} e^{-\tau_{2} \mu_{2}^{2} / 2}\right) \frac{\sqrt{2 \pi} e^{\left(\tau_{2} \mu_{2}+\tau_{1}\left(x-\mu_{1}\right)\right)^{2} /\left(2 \tau_{2}+2 \tau_{1}\right)}}{\sqrt{\tau_{2}+\tau_{1}}} \\
&=\sqrt{\frac{\frac{\tau_{1} \tau_{2}}{\tau_{1}+\tau_{2}}}{2 \pi}} \exp \left(-\left(\frac{\tau_{1} \tau_{2}}{\tau_{1}+\tau_{2}}\right)\left(x-\mu_{1}-\mu_{2}\right)^{2} / 2\right)\end{aligned}$$
